Some data to be noted

According to the World Health Organisation (WHO), an estimated 36 million people worldwide are blind and an additional 217 million people have moderate to severe visual impairment. The vast majority of these people (over 80%) live in low-income settings and many do not have access to basic eye care services. In the future, the number of people who are blind or visually impaired is expected to increase in the coming decades, partly due to an ageing population and an increase in chronic diseases such as diabetes, which can lead to vision loss. Despite significant advances in assistive technology in recent years, such as screen readers and tactile displays, and increased awareness aimed at improving accessibility and inclusion, we still need deeper technological innovation in how to promote their well-being.

The only thing worse than being blind is having sight but no vision. Helen Keller

Difficulties faced by blind people

Blind people may experience difficulties in terms of accessibility, employment, education and facing discrimination. But often physical movement is the most fundamental behaviour, and regular walking and exercise can help blind people develop and maintain their orientation and mobility skills, which are essential for navigating their environment safely and independently. Walking and exercise also have a positive impact on mental health, reducing stress and anxiety, and can provide opportunities for blind people to socialise with others, meet new people and participate in group activities.

I am not my disability. I am a person, first and foremost. Marla Runyan

The importance of the white cane

The white cane plays an important role in the lives of blind and visually impaired people, helping them to navigate the world and assert their right to access public spaces and services. Used to detect obstacles and navigate their environment, it helps them to walk safely and confidently and to navigate over obstacles such as curbs, steps and uneven terrain. In addition to its practical use, the white cane is an important symbol of identity and community for blind people.The white cane is also considered to be a symbol of the disability rights movement and its use is protected by law in many countries.

The white cane is not just a mobility tool, it's a badge of independence. Mary Hiland
